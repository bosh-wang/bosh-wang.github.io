      A second type of empennage design does not require an

      sensitive. The antiservo tab also functions as a trim tab to
      relieve control pressures and helps maintain the stabilator in
      the desired position.

  • Induced drag
    • How does induced drag generate?
      • When airplane fly forward, the air pressure on top of airfoil is lower than below ‚Üí it will make air below flow upwards
      • Creating vortices like this
    • Thus, the ‚Äúdownwash‚Äù airstream will create an induced drag
    • AOA and induced drag
      • In any case, as AOA increases, induced drag increases proportionally.
      • The lower the airspeed, the greater the AOA required to produce lift, and thus the more induced drag.
    • Relation between airspeed and drag
      • When airspeed increases ‚Üí parasite drag increases, induced drag decreases
      • When airspeed decreases ‚Üí parasite drag decreases, induced drag increases
    • Ground effect
      • When altitude is low, the induced drag will decrease compared to high altitude.
      • Thus, less speed the create the same lift when near the ground.

Wingtip Vortices

  • Whenever airfoil is producing lift, the induced drag occurs and wingtip vortices are created.
    • The heavier and slower the aircraft, the greater the AOA and the stronger the wingtip vortices.
    • Thus, an aircraft will create wingtip vortices with maximum strength occurring during the takeoff, climb, and landing phases of flight.
    • Creating a hazard called ‚Äúwake turbulence‚Äù
  • Avoiding wake turbulence
